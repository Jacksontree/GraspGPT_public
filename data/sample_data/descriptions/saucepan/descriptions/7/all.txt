The shape of a saucepan is typically circular with sloping sides that gradually widen towards the circumference of the rim. The base is usually rounded with a handle or two attached to it. A saucepan is a type of kitchenware used for cooking that has a rounded bottom, a long handle, and typically two pour spouts for liquids. It is used to heat liquids, most commonly stews, sauces, and soups. A saucepan has a cylindrical body with a slightly conical base and a wide, flat rim, so other objects that have similar geometries include water bottles, cans, and jars. A saucepan is a deep, broad, usually round vessel with a handle. Household objects that are comparable in function are stockpots, Dutch ovens and frying pans.
