Verbs that achieve similar effects to 'pour an object' include decant, siphon, and decant. Decanting involves pouring a liquid from one container to another, such as a bottle of wine being poured into a decanter to increase the surface area and oxygen exposure. Siphoning involves the use of suction to draw a liquid from one level to another, such as drawing gasoline from a lower tank to a higher tank. Dispense requires the use of pressure to dispense a liquid from a container, like pressing down on a ketchup bottle to dispense ketchup onto a hamburger.
